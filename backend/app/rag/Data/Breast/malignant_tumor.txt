Malignant breast tumors represent cancerous growths that invade surrounding tissues and can metastasize, primarily invasive ductal carcinoma and lobular carcinoma, requiring prompt multimodal treatment.
Definition
Malignant breast tumors are invasive cancerous neoplasms originating from ductal or lobular epithelium, capable of local invasion and distant metastasis via lymphatics or bloodstream. Invasive ductal carcinoma (IDC, 70-80%) forms firm masses breaking through basement membrane; invasive lobular carcinoma (ILC, 10-15%) shows Indian file pattern; subtypes include triple-negative, HER2+, and hormone-positive, graded by Nottingham criteria.clevelandclinic+1
Symptoms
Symptoms include hard, irregular fixed lumps (often painless), nipple retraction/discharge (bloody), skin dimpling/peau d'orange, axillary swelling, or inflammatory changes (redness/swelling in IBC). Advanced features: bone pain, weight loss, dyspnea from lung/liver mets; 90% present as palpable mass in upper outer quadrant.mayoclinic+1
Causes
Malignant transformation stems from cumulative genetic/epigenetic hits (BRCA1/2, TP53, PIK3CA mutations) disrupting apoptosis/proliferation, amplified by estrogen exposure, HER2 overexpression, and microenvironment changes. Sporadic (90%) vs. hereditary (10%); radiation/dense breasts accelerate.bcrf+1
Risk Factors
Key risks: female sex, age >50, BRCA mutations (lifetime risk 50-85%), dense breasts, nulliparity, late menopause, HRT/alcohol, obesity (postmenopausal), family history, prior chest radiation. Protective: oophorectomy, breastfeeding >1yr (4%/yr risk reduction).clevelandclinic+1
Diagnosis
Diagnosis via triple test: exam, imaging (mammogram asymmetry/microcalcifications, US, MRI), core biopsy (ER/PR/HER2 IHC, Ki-67). Staging: TNM/sentinel node/PET-CT; Oncotype DX predicts recurrence; 80% sensitivity mammography.mskcc+1
Treatment
Multimodal: surgery (lumpectomy/mastectomy ± reconstruction), radiation (post-lumpectomy), neoadjuvant/adjuvant chemo (taxanes/anthracyclines), endocrine (tamoxifen/AIs for ER+), HER2-targeted (trastuzumab), CDK4/6 (palbociclib), PARP (olaparib BRCA+), immunotherapy (pembrolizumab TNBC).who+1
Prevention
Prevention: risk-reducing mastectomy/salpingo-oophorectomy (BRCA), chemoprevention (tamoxifen/raloxifene RR=0.5 high-risk), screening (mammograms 40+, MRI dense/BRCA), lifestyle (limit alcohol, exercise, BMI<25). No universal vaccine; early detection cures 99% stage I.mayoclinic+1
