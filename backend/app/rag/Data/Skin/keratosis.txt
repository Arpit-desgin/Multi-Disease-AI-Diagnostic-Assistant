Keratosis refers to thickened, horny growths of the outer skin layer (epidermis), with common types including actinic keratosis (precancerous), seborrheic keratosis (benign), and keratosis pilaris (follicular plugs).
Definition
Keratosis encompasses abnormal skin conditions with hyperkeratosis (excess keratin buildup), including actinic keratosis (sun-induced precancerous lesions), seborrheic keratosis (common benign "stuck-on" warty plaques in older adults), and keratosis pilaris (rough follicular papules on arms/thighs). Actinic keratoses carry 5-10% risk of progressing to squamous cell carcinoma if untreated; others are cosmetic.
Symptoms
Symptoms vary: actinic keratosis shows rough, scaly, red/brown patches (1-2cm) on sun-exposed areas (face, scalp, hands) with tenderness/itching; seborrheic keratosis appears as waxy, brown-black "stuck-on" bumps with greasy scales; keratosis pilaris causes small, rough, goosebump-like plugs (red/flesh-colored) on upper arms/thighs/cheeks, often dry/itchy. Bleeding/crusting signals progression.
Causes
Actinic keratosis stems from chronic UV damage causing atypical keratinocyte dysplasia; seborrheic keratosis from benign epidermal proliferation (genetic/age-related); keratosis pilaris from keratin/fatty deposit plugs blocking hair follicles, linked to atopic dermatitis/ichthyosis. UV exposure drives actinic type; no infectious cause.
Risk Factors
Risk factors include fair skin, prolonged sun exposure/UV tanning (actinic), older age (>40), family history/genetics (seborrheic), atopy/dry skin (pilaris), immunosuppression, and outdoor occupations. Actinic risk highest in Fitzpatrick types I-II; seborrheic nearly universal after 50; pilaris common in children/teens with asthma/eczema.
Diagnosis
Diagnosis is clinical via dermoscopy/exam: actinic (strawberry-like red scales), seborrheic (stuck-on, comedo-like plugs), pilaris (follicular roughness). Biopsy confirms atypia (actinic) or acanthosis (seborrheic); differentiate from warts, SCC, melanoma; Wood's lamp aids pilaris.
Treatment
Actinic: cryotherapy (liquid N2), topical 5-FU/imiquimod/ingenol mebutate, photodynamic therapy, curettage. Seborrheic: cryotherapy, shave excision, laser/electrosurgery (cosmetic). Pilaris: keratolytics (urea/lactic acid), topical retinoids, moisturizers; no cure, symptom relief. Monitor actinic sites.
Prevention
Prevention: broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily, hats/clothing 10am-4pm (actinic), gentle exfoliation/moisturizing (pilaris), avoid tanning beds. Annual skin exams for high-risk; emollients for pilaris; early treatment prevents actinic progression to cancer.
