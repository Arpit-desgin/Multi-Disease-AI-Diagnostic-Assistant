Proliferative diabetic retinopathy (PDR) represents the most advanced stage where retinal ischemia triggers fragile neovascularization, posing high risk of vitreous hemorrhage and tractional retinal detachment.
Definition
Proliferative diabetic retinopathy (ETDRS level 60+) features neovascularization of the disc (NVD) or elsewhere (NVE >1/2 disc area), vitreous/preretinal hemorrhage, or fibrovascular proliferation. New vessels grow from ischemic retina due to VEGF surge; high-risk characteristics (NVD, NVE >1/2 DD, or preretinal hemorrhage) predict 50% severe vision loss within 1 year without treatment.mayoclinic+1
Symptoms
Floaters from vitreous hemorrhage, sudden painless vision loss, peripheral field defects; advanced cases show rubeosis iridis (neovascular glaucoma), tractional retinal detachment symptoms (distorted vision, shadows). 30% present with vitreous bleed obscuring fundus view.mayoclinic
Causes
Severe retinal ischemia from extensive capillary non-perfusion triggers hypoxia-inducible VEGF, angiopoietin-2, and placental growth factor, stimulating abnormal vessel proliferation on disc, elsewhere, or iris. 15-20 years poor glycemic control typical.ncbi.nlm.nih
Risk Factors
HbA1c >9.5%, diabetes >15 years, nephropathy (50% concurrent), pregnancy (rapid progression), cataract surgery (angle neovascularization risk). Type 2 patients have 2x PDR prevalence vs. type 1.ncbi.nlm.nih
Diagnosis
Dilated exam reveals neovascular fronds (fine lacy loops), preretinal/vitreous blood, fibrous traction; B-scan ultrasound if media opaque; FA confirms leakage/non-perfusion (>10 disc areas ischemia = PDR threshold). UWF imaging detects 90% peripheral NVE missed on standard 7-field.eyewiki+1
Treatment
Urgent retina referral: Panretinal photocoagulation (PRP, 1200-2000 spots) or anti-VEGF (ranibizumab/aflibercept q4-8w) per DRCR Protocol S (anti-VEGF noninferior to PRP, fewer vitrectomies). Vitrectomy indicated for non-clearing vitreous hemorrhage >1 month, tractional detachment involving macula. Rubeosis requires IOP-lowering + PRP/anti-VEGF.pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih+1
Prevention
Tight glycemic/BP/lipid control prevents 50% PDR development; fenofibrate (FIELD study) reduces progression 30%; very severe NPDR PRP consideration prevents 60% PDR. Quarterly screening in severe NPDR detects 95% pre-PDR.ncbi.nlm.nih
